What is Sentiment Analysis?

Sentiment analysis is the process of using natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning to determine the emotional tone behind text data. It helps classify text as positive, negative, or neutral.

Generally, sentiment analysis is a tool that helps us understand the overall feeling or opinion behind a bunch of text, whether it's movie reviews, customer feedback, or social media posts. It's like having a mood detector for words! This can be really useful for businesses to see what people think about their products, for politicians to understand public opinion, or even just for you to get a quick summary of what people are saying about something online.

How it works

Our project predicts mental health conditions using social media data through Sentiment Analysis with SVM (Support Vector Machine) and BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers).

1.SVM (Support Vector Machine):

  • A machine learning algorithm that classifies text as positive, negative, or neutral based on predefined features.
  • Works well with structured text data and smaller datasets.
  • Uses mathematical functions to separate different sentiment categories.

2. BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers):

  • A pre-trained deep learning model that understands the meaning of words based on their context.
  • Considers both previous and next words in a sentence for better accuracy.
  • Helps detect complex emotions like sarcasm and hidden distress.

3. Logistic Regression:

  • A statistical machine learning model used for binary and multi-class classification.
  • Uses TF-IDF to convert text into numerical features before classification.
  • Calculates the probability of sentiment categories and selects the one with the highest score.
  • Efficient, interpretable, and useful for detecting basic sentiment trends.
